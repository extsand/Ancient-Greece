Traditionally, the Olympics include twelve gods. Mainly from the third and fourth generations of divine beings "titans (titanids)" described in Greek mythology, the so-called "Twelve Gods" (Δωδεκάθεον). At the same time, the composition of the "twelve" was not canonical in ancient Greek theology. Depending on the local cults, the composition of the twelve-member pantheon changed. In particular, there were twelve, thirteen, and fourteen such gods at different times.
